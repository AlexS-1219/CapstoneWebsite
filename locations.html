Potential Pilot Cities for Pathogen Detection System

Why Airports?

Airports serve as major transportation hubs with a constant flow of passengers, baggage, and cargo. The sheer volume of people and goods passing through airports daily presents significant security challenges. Implementing advanced detection technology like AI coupled with electrospray technology can help manage and screen this high volume efficiently, ensuring thorough security checks without causing significant disruptions to travel schedules.

Implementing this in airports would act as an early warning system. By continuously monitoring air quality and analyzing samples collected through electrospray technology, airports can establish an early warning system for detecting potential chemical or biological threats. This proactive approach allows for swift responses to emerging security concerns, enhancing overall airport safety.

Why These Locations?

High Population Density: These cities are among the most populous in the United States, with millions of residents and visitors. The high population density increases the risk of security threats, and disease transmission.

Key Transportation Hubs: All of these cities are major transportation hubs with international airports, seaports, and extensive public transportation networks.

Tourism and Cultural Landmarks: These cities are popular tourist destinations with iconic landmarks, cultural attractions, and entertainment venues.

Where specifically in airports?

Security checkpoints: This is the primary location where passengers and their belongings undergo screening before boarding flights.

Baggage screening areas: In addition to screening passengers, airports also inspect checked baggage for security threats.

Cargo screening facilities: Airports handle a significant volume of cargo transported via passenger and cargo aircraft

Airside security zones: Areas beyond security checkpoints, such as boarding gates and secure airside zones, are critical for maintaining the integrity of the airport perimeter and preventing unauthorized access to aircraft.

Public areas and entry points: while the primary focus is on securing passenger and cargo pathways, deploying this technology at strategic entry points and public areas within the airport can provide an added layer of security against potential threats entering the facility.
